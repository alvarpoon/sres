“Upturn in letting activity in 2014 is expected.”
Research Savills
  • The economy should continue to steadily recover. GDP increased slightly in 2013 (+0.2%) and forecasts are for +0.8% in 2014.
  • 2.1m sq m of take-up is expected in the Ile-de-France region in 2014. 2013’s weak performance should be followed by an upturn in letting activity, including the return to the table of deals exceeding 5,000 sq m, driven by companies looking to consolidate space.
  • 2014 will be a year of general improvement for the market, but with strong contrasts—with Paris’s CBD and the Western Crescent remaining key markets in terms of values and transaction volumes.
  • The downward spiral in rental levels appears to have come to an end. Some users, attracted by the new letting terms, may consider a return to the main business areas of the capital.
