  • A slight district-wide drop in rents was witnessed in all submarkets for the first time since Q2/2009. Expansion demand remained weak in Q4 and vacancies across all submarkets except Central rose at a rate of 0.1 to 1.8 percentage points.
  • Savills expects the Grade A office market to decline by 5% in 2014, as the global economic recovery is still uncertain and firms remain wary of expanding or increasing headcount, leading to weak demand from the general business environment in Hong Kong.
  • In Central, the decline in Grade A office rents decelerated to 1.3% in Q4, 0.7 of a ppt less than in Q3, and resulted in a 6.4% decline in rents for 2013 as a whole and an accumulated decline of 21% since the most recent peak in the second half of 2011.
