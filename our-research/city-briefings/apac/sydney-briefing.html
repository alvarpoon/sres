“Savills expect the recovery phase to continue over the coming year with moderate rental growth over the medium-term as tenants in the Sydney CBD market start to feel the pinch from a lack of supply.”
  • The latest numbers from PCA indicate that overall vacancy has increased slightly to 9%, up from 8.9% six months earlier.
  • Supply remains severely constrained over the short term with 47,486 square metres of net supply due to complete in 2014.
  • Full floor availability as at February 2014 is currently sitting at 13.8%, with eight available options for tenants >10,000 square metres.
  • Net absorption of 6,305 square metres was recorded in the 12 months to December 2013.
  • Sales activity in the 12 months to March 2014 totalled $2.9 billion, almost double the amount recorded in the prior 12 month period.
