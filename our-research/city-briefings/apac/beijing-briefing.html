“High rents, a slowing economy, and weak demand for expansions and new leases resulted in net take-up falling to a decade low in 2013.”
Joan Wang, Savills Research
  • One new project, the 150,000- sq m Fortune Financial Centre, was handed over in Q4/2013, bringing city-wide leasable stock to 9.34 million sq m.
  • Despite stable occupancy rates in existing buildings, the sizable new supply pushed the city-wide vacancy rate up by 0.9 of a percentage point (ppt) quarter-on-quarter (QoQ) to 4.4.
  • Grade A office rents fell for the third consecutive quarter by 0.6% QoQ to an average of RMB313.0 per sq m per month, down 1.6% year-on-year (YoY).
  • Nine projects are scheduled to launch in 2014 adding nearly 600,000 sq m of leasable office GFA. The ensuing rise in the city-wide vacancy rate and stronger landlord competition is expected to force rents down by more than 2% by year’s end.
